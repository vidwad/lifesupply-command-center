# 11 - Reporting Requirements

**Project:** LifeSupply Command Center  
**Document status:** Batch 2 post-MVP requirements  
**Prepared:** May 10, 2026  
**Primary audience:** Claude Code, developers, finance team, management, product owner

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the reporting requirements for the LifeSupply Command Center after the MVP stage.

The reporting engine should help LifeSupply produce consistent management, operational, financial, marketing, supplier, board, investor, lender, financing, and strategic opportunity reports from the same controlled data foundation.

The reporting layer should reduce manual preparation time while improving consistency, transparency, and decision usefulness.

---

## 2. Reporting Strategy

Reports should be generated from normalized data stored in the Command Center, with references to source systems such as BigCommerce, QuickBooks Online, Mailchimp, GA4, supplier automation logs, uploaded files, and management adjustments.

AI may be used to draft commentary, but reports should clearly distinguish:

- Source data.
- Management calculations.
- AI-generated commentary.
- Human-approved commentary.
- Draft vs approved status.

---

8. Supplier performance reports.
9. Customer reactivation reports.
10. Marketing performance reports.
11. Website analytics reports.
12. AI briefing reports.
13. M&A opportunity reports.
14. Strategic opportunity reports.
15. Exception and task reports.

---

## 4. Report Lifecycle

Reports should follow a controlled lifecycle.

Suggested states:

- Draft.
- Generated.
- Pending review.
- Approved.
- Distributed.
- Revised.
- Superseded.
- Archived.

### Required Metadata

Every generated report should include:

- Report name.
- Report type.
- Reporting period.
- Generated date and time.
- Generated by user or system.
- Data sources used.
- Source sync timestamps.
- Approval status.
- Approved by.
- Version number.
- Export format.

---

## 5. Report Repository

The Command Center should include a report repository where users can find prior reports.

### Repository Features

- Search by report type.
- Filter by period.
- Filter by approval status.
- Filter by business unit.
- View report history.
- Download PDF.
- Download Excel/CSV where available.
- View AI commentary version.
- View human-approved version.
- See source data timestamps.

## 6. Daily Operating Brief

### Purpose

Give management a concise daily overview of what requires attention.

### Frequency

Daily, generated automatically or manually.

### Audience

- Executive / Owner.
- Operations Manager.
- Finance Manager, limited financial content if configured.
- Product Manager.

### Sections

1. Executive summary.
2. Prior-day sales.
3. Month-to-date sales.
4. Open orders.
5. Delayed orders.
6. Supplier exceptions.
7. Product and margin alerts.
8. Marketing and website highlights.
9. Urgent tasks.
10. Recommended priorities.

### Data Sources

- BigCommerce.
- Supplier automation logs.
- Task system.
- Product database.
- Mailchimp.
- GA4.
- QuickBooks summaries, if available.

### Sections

1. Executive summary.
2. Consolidated revenue.

4. Revenue by geography.
5. Gross profit.
6. Gross margin.
7. Operating expenses.
8. EBITDA and adjusted EBITDA.

- Revenue and margin highlights.

## 19. PDF Export Requirements

PDF reports should be professional, readable, and consistent.

### Requirements

- Cover/title page where appropriate.
- Date and reporting period.
- Page numbers.
- Company branding.
- Executive summary.
- Tables with clean formatting.
- Charts with labels.
- Footnotes for unaudited or management-prepared data.
- Appendix schedules.
- Version and approval metadata.

---

## 20. Excel / CSV Export Requirements

Exported schedules should support:

- Underlying data export.
- Report summary tables.
- Product-level data.
- Customer segment data.
- Supplier performance data.
- Financial schedules.
- Campaign data.
- Variance analysis data.

Export files should include generated timestamp and source data period.

---

## 21. AI Commentary Rules

AI commentary can be included in reports, but must follow these rules:

- AI text is draft until approved.
- AI must use report data inputs, not invent figures.
- AI should state data limitations.
- Financial commentary must identify unaudited data where applicable.
- Investor-facing commentary requires Executive approval.
- Board-facing commentary requires Executive or Finance approval.

---

## 22. Report Data Source Control

Reports should show the source systems used.

Example:

```text
Data Sources:
- BigCommerce orders synchronized through May 10, 2026 at 6:00 AM Pacific.
- QuickBooks Online financial summaries through April 30, 2026, not yet externally reviewed.
- Mailchimp campaign data synchronized May 10, 2026 at 6:15 AM Pacific.
- GA4 data synchronized May 10, 2026 at 6:20 AM Pacific.
```

---

## 24. Acceptance Criteria

The reporting system is acceptable when:

- Reports can be generated by type and period.
- Reports show source data timestamps.
- Reports support draft and approved states.
- AI commentary can be reviewed and edited.
- PDF export works for core reports.
- CSV/XLSX export works for underlying schedules.
- Financial reports distinguish QuickBooks data from management adjustments.
- Investor and board reports require approval before distribution.
- Report history is searchable.
